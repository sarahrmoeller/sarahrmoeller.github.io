My research encompasses an interdisciplinary approach that builds bridges between computer science and linguistics, building a virtuous cycle of mutual benefit to the documentation, scientific study, and revitalization of minority languages. I apply computational methods to the analytic description of under-documented languages while aiming to improve natural language processing (NLP) in low-resource settings.

I have a keen interest in bridging the technological gap between NLP and "pen-and-paper" linguistics. My special interest has long been minority languages of the former Soviet Union, where I first encountered language endangerment and later did fieldwork with Nakh-Daghestanian languages. My research leads to interesting questions about how best practices in linguistics field methods might change as we encounter modern AI. It also opens doors for training non-computational linguists and language community members about AI and how to leverage it to their benefit.

Before entering academia, I taught English as a Foreign Language for many years, did freelance Russian-English interpreting, and worked in the NLP industry. You can read one version of my journey here. I love helping linguists or others from humanities backgrounds to get started with computational methods. Check out my FAQs!
